What Is RPO and When Should Your Organization Use It?

Recruitment Process Outsourcing explained — the different models, the business case, and how RPO compares to traditional staffing and internal talent acquisition.

Masota Recruiting Team • December 2025

What Is Recruitment Process Outsourcing?

Recruitment Process Outsourcing (RPO) is an arrangement in which an employer transfers all or part of its recruiting function to an external provider. Unlike traditional staffing — where the firm fills individual requisitions — RPO is a deeper partnership where the provider takes ownership of the process, the metrics, and often the employer brand experience for candidates.

RPO Models

RPO ranges from end-to-end outsourcing of the entire talent acquisition function to selective support for specific elements of the process.

  • Full RPO — the provider manages all recruiting including sourcing, screening, assessment, offer, and onboarding coordination
  • Selective RPO — the provider handles specific high-volume roles or departments while the internal team manages the rest
  • Project RPO — a defined, time-limited engagement to address a specific hiring surge or backlog

When RPO Makes Sense

RPO is most valuable when an organization is growing rapidly and internal recruiting capacity cannot scale quickly enough, when hiring volume is high and consistent, or when the internal recruiting team has capability gaps in specific areas. It can also be the right solution when an organization wants to dramatically reduce time-to-fill and cost-per-hire metrics across the board.

How RPO Compares to Traditional Staffing

Traditional staffing fills individual requisitions on a contingency or retained basis. RPO is a structural engagement that takes ownership of the recruiting process itself. The right choice depends on hiring volume, consistency, and the degree to which you want your recruiting function managed versus supplemented externally.
